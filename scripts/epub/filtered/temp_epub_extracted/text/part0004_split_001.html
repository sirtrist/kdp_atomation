He made a slight sign to Razumihin to stop his consolatory eloquence, and, taking his mother and sister by the hand, he gazed at them alternately for two minutes without speaking. His look, full of painful sensibility, had at the same time something fixed and senseless in it. Pulcheria Alexandrovna was frightened and began to cry.
