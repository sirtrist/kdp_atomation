The morning

The day was not fine. The drops of water made a sad noise on my panes; dark in my room, dark outside. My head was spinning, I had a fever.

– A letter for you, my little father, the postillion is bringing it, he said Matryona .

"From whom?" I asked, without knowing what I was saying.

– How should I know, my little father? Read for yourself.

I broke the seal.

"Oh! Forgive me. I beg you on my knees to forgive me; I did not want to deceive you, and yet I have deceived you. Forgive me! Yet I have not changed for you, I loved you, I still love you; why are you not him?

I read and reread this letter for a long time; at last it fell from my hands, and I hid my face.

– My little father, said Matrona .

– What, old woman?

– I've taken down all the cobwebs, all of them; if you want to get married now, the house is clean.

I looked Matryona . She was a woman still fairly well preserved, rather young, but why did her gaze seem so extinguished, her face so wrinkled, her shoulders so stooped, the whole creature so decrepit? And why did it seem to me that the room had aged like the old woman? The walls and the floor were dull, and spider webs! there were more than ever. Everything was dark... yes, I had before me the prospect of my future, sad, sad, oh! sad. I saw myself that day as I am today fifteen years later, in the same room, with the same Matryona who has no more imagination than in the past.
